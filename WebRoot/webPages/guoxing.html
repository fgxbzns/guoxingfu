Research

Research Areas: Structural biology, Systems biology and Bioinformatics

  • Caspases: Caspases are major players in apoptosis (programmed cell death). Dysfunction of caspase activity is associated with many human diseases. Excessive apoptosis will lead to stroke and neurodegenerative diseases (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's disease). Insufficient apoptosis is invoved in cancer and autoimmune diseases. We have investigated the substrate specificity of executioner caspase at P5 position in substrates. Summary

  • D-arginine dehydrogenase: D-arginine dehydrogenase is involved in the D-to-L inversion of arginine in Pseudomonas aeruginosa. It contains one molecule of FAD as a cofactor. We have determined the first crystal structure of this enzyme and studied its recognition of D-amino acids. Summary

  • Deterministic-stochastic crossover method: The deterministic-stochastic crossover algorithm is a novel method for simulating biological networks. It retains the high efficiency of deterministic method while still reflects the stochastic effects of random fluctuations of the system at lower concentration. We have introduced XML formatted model, multiple compartments and system event into this algorithm, making it a powerful tool for simulating biochemical systems. Web Server
